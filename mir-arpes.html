The Lanzara Lab recently expanded our tabletop pump-probe ARPES capabilities to include a wider range of pump excitation channels. The new system combines a powerful, amplified Ti:Sapphire laser with optical parametric amplification and difference frequency generation (OPA+DFG). The result is a tunable mid-infrared light source with a state-of-the-art pulse energy range of 50-150 uJ for wavelengths between 4-15 um. This translates to large excitation fields, on order 10 MV/cm. The same laser also feeds a four-wave mixing system to produce 200 nm (6.2 eV) ultraviolet light as a photoemission probe.
